Facts About Cats

Cats have a remarkable sense of hearing and can detect higher frequencies than humans or even dogs. While humans can hear frequencies up to 20,000 Hz, cats can hear sounds as high as 64,000 Hz! This extraordinary hearing ability helps them hunt small prey, like rodents, that make high-pitched noises. Interestingly, cats also recognize their owners’ voices but might choose to ignore them because they are independent creatures. They respond better to high-pitched sounds or whistles, so if you’re trying to call your cat, you may want to adjust your tone!

The cat is a small, carnivorous mammal and a domesticated descendant of wild felines. Also known as the domestic cat, it is believed to have descended from the African wildcat (Felis silvestris lybica). Cats were first domesticated by humans around 9,000 years ago, primarily in the Near East, where they helped protect grain stores from rodents. Their independent nature and hunting skills made them valuable companions to early agricultural societies.
